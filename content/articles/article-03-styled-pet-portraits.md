## The Classical Collection: Timeless Elegance

<!-- IMAGE: Side-by-side comparison showing a cat in the Royal style and the same cat in the Johannes Vermeer style, highlighting the different lighting and compositional approaches -->

### Royal

Imagine your pet stepping out of the frame of a painting that hangs in the Louvre. The Royal style places your pet in rich velvet robes against a classical oil painting backdrop, complete with gilded frame details that seem to extend beyond the image boundary.

**What you'll see:** Dramatic Baroque lighting that sculpts your pet's features with chiaroscuro shadows. Deep crimson, royal blue, and gold fabrics that convey wealth and status. An ornate golden frame rendered as part of the image itself. Your pet's expression becomes regal, dignified—worthy of a monarch's court portrait.

**Best for:** Formal living rooms, statement pieces above fireplaces, pet parents who appreciate traditional art

### Johannes Vermeer

The Dutch Master known for "Girl with a Pearl Earring" had a singular gift for capturing soft, diffused window light. This style recreates that luminous quality with your pet as the subject.

**What you'll see:** Gentle illumination from the left side, creating subtle shadows that give your pet three-dimensional presence. A dark, neutral background that makes your pet's fur seem to glow. Pearl-like highlights in the eyes. An intimate, domestic atmosphere that feels like a private moment captured forever.

**Best for:** Bedroom art, quiet spaces, cat owners (this style particularly flatters feline subjects)

### Embroidery Art

Textile art meets digital precision in this unique style that transforms your pet into a stitched masterpiece.

**What you'll see:** Visible thread-work patterns that follow the contours of your pet's form. Fabric texture that suggests linen or canvas backing. Individual stitches defining whiskers, fur direction, and facial features. A handcrafted aesthetic that belies its digital origin.

**Best for:** Cottage-style decor, craft room art, anyone who appreciates fiber arts

## The Contemporary Collection: Modern Aesthetics

<!-- IMAGE: Three-panel display showing the Wes Anderson Pop, Magazine Chic, and Smart Casual styles with a dog subject, demonstrating the range of modern aesthetics -->

### Wes Anderson Pop

The distinctive visual language of director Wes Anderson—symmetrical compositions, pastel palettes, whimsical narrative quality—translated into pet portraiture.

**What you'll see:** Perfectly centered composition with your pet facing directly forward. Soft pink, mint green, butter yellow, and powder blue backgrounds. A storybook quality that suggests your pet is a character in a charming indie film. Props and environmental details that hint at narrative without overwhelming the subject.

**Best for:** Social media content, children's rooms, anyone with an appreciation for quirky aesthetics

### Magazine Chic

High-fashion editorial photography reimagined for pet subjects. This is your pet as cover star.

**What you'll see:** Dramatic studio lighting with carefully placed highlights and shadows. A sophisticated, slightly aloof expression that channels supermodel energy. Clean backgrounds in neutral tones. The kind of image that wouldn't look out of place in Vogue or Harper's Bazaar—if they ran pet editorials.

**Best for:** Modern interiors, fashion-forward pet parents, statement pieces

### Smart Casual

Your pet at their most approachable—dressed well but not formally, captured with the clean aesthetic of a contemporary lifestyle brand.

**What you'll see:** Tasteful everyday attire (a bandana, a simple collar, perhaps a casual sweater). Natural, relaxed poses. Clean backgrounds in soft white or gray. The visual equivalent of your pet being ready for brunch or a casual Friday at the office.

**Best for:** Entryway art, casual living spaces, capturing your pet's everyday personality

## The Playful Collection: Bold and Expressive

<!-- IMAGE: Vibrant display of Retro Pop Art, Music Lover, and Birthday styles showing the bold colors and energetic compositions -->

### Retro Pop Art

The 1960s advertising aesthetic of Roy Lichtenstein and Andy Warhol, applied to your furry subject.

**What you'll see:** Bold Ben-Day dots creating shading and texture. Thick black outlines defining every form. Saturated primary colors—red, yellow, blue, plus hot pink and lime green. A graphic, poster-like quality that demands attention. Comic book energy translated into fine art.

**Best for:** Game rooms, home offices, anyone who loves bold color and graphic design

### Music Lover

Your pet as rock star, jazz musician, or classical virtuoso—complete with instruments and stage presence.

**What you'll see:** Your pet holding or positioned with musical instruments—guitars, saxophones, pianos. Stage lighting effects suggesting a live performance. Either formal concert attire or rock-and-roll casual wear. The attitude of a musician who knows they're the main event.

**Best for:** Music rooms, creative studios, pet parents who are musicians

### Birthday

Pure celebration captured in visual form—confetti, balloons, and unbridled joy.

**What you'll see:** Festive party atmosphere with streamers and confetti. A party hat or festive accessory on your pet. Bright, cheerful colors that evoke celebration. The visual equivalent of a surprise party in full swing.

**Best for:** Commemorating adoption days, actual birthdays, seasonal greeting cards

## The Color Collection: Mood Through Palette

### Emerald Muse

Deep, rich greens dominate this moody, sophisticated style that evokes enchanted forests and precious gems.

**What you'll see:** Your pet emerging from or surrounded by deep emerald tones. Gold accents that complement the green. A mysterious, slightly magical atmosphere. The color of growth, renewal, and nature's abundance applied as artistic filter.

**Best for:** Studies, libraries, spaces with green or gold accent colors

### Bordeaux Muse

The deep red of fine wine translated into artistic atmosphere—warm, rich, and intoxicating.

**What you'll see:** Burgundy and crimson tones creating warmth and depth. Your pet rendered in the colors of autumn and aged wine. A cozy, intimate feeling that suggests evenings by the fire. Romantic without being saccharine.

**Best for:** Dining rooms, warm-toned spaces, fall and winter seasonal decor

### Christmas Vibe

The holiday spirit distilled into visual form—not just Christmas colors, but Christmas *feeling*.

**What you'll see:** Red and green elements integrated naturally into the composition. Soft snowfall or twinkling lights in the background. Your pet in festive attire or surrounded by seasonal elements. The warmth and nostalgia of holiday memories.

**Best for:** Holiday cards, seasonal home decor, December social media content

## Choosing Your Style: A Decision Framework

<!-- IMAGE: Flowchart-style infographic showing decision paths: "Is your decor traditional? → Royal or Vermeer. Love bold colors? → Retro Pop Art. Want something playful? → Wes Anderson or Birthday" -->

With 12 distinct options, how do you choose? Consider these factors:

**Your Home's Aesthetic:**
- Traditional/traditional transitional → Royal, Vermeer, Embroidery Art
- Modern/contemporary → Magazine Chic, Smart Casual
- Eclectic/playful → Wes Anderson Pop, Retro Pop Art
- Moody/sophisticated → Emerald Muse, Bordeaux Muse

**Your Pet's Personality:**
- Dignified and calm → Royal, Vermeer
- Playful and energetic → Birthday, Music Lover
- Quirky and unique → Wes Anderson Pop
- Confident and bold → Retro Pop Art, Magazine Chic

**The Intended Use:**
- Gallery wall centerpiece → Royal, Vermeer, Magazine Chic
- Social media content → Wes Anderson Pop, Retro Pop Art, Birthday
- Gift → Consider the recipient's taste, or choose Smart Casual as a safe elegant option
- Seasonal/holiday → Christmas Vibe, Birthday
